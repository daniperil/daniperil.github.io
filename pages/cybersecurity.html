Cybersecurity and the CIA Triad

Cybersecurity is the practice of protecting computer systems, networks, and sensitive information from unauthorized access, use, disclosure, disruption, modification, or destruction. It involves the use of technologies, processes, and policies to ensure the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of digital assets.

The CIA Triad is a model used to describe the three key principles of information security: Confidentiality, Integrity, and Availability.

  • Confidentiality: Ensuring that information is accessible only to those authorized to have access.
  • Integrity: Maintaining the accuracy and completeness of information and preventing unauthorized modification or destruction.
  • Availability: Ensuring that information is accessible and usable when needed, and that systems are operating reliably and efficiently.
